# Blog Photo Shot List: Artwork Prep Guide

## Priority Photos (Critical for Article)

### 1. **File Format Comparison** (Hero Section)
**What to shoot:**
- Side-by-side comparison showing the same design as:
  - Vector file (clean, sharp edges)
  - High-res raster (300 DPI, still looks good)
  - Low-res image (72 DPI, pixelated when zoomed)

**How to shoot:**
- Open all three versions in design software
- Zoom to 400% on each
- Screenshot all three
- Place side-by-side in one image

**Photo specs:** 1200×600px, landscape orientation

---

### 2. **Resolution Check in Photoshop** (Resolution Section)
**What to shoot:**
- Screenshot of Photoshop's "Image Size" dialog
- Show dimensions, resolution field (300 pixels/inch)
- Circle or highlight the resolution number

**Alternative:** Screen recording showing how to navigate to Image → Image Size

**Photo specs:** 800×600px, screenshot with annotations

---

### 3. **Screen Printing Press in Action** (Throughout Article)
**What to shoot:**
- Close-up of ink being pulled across a screen
- Show the squeegee, mesh screen, and ink
- Print mid-process showing one color being laid down
- Final multi-color print being lifted off the press

**Lighting:** Bright overhead lighting, avoid shadows
**Angle:** 45-degree angle from side, capture the action

**Photo specs:** 1200×800px, landscape, high resolution

---

### 4. **Print Quality Comparison** (Resolution Section)
**What to shoot:**
- Two printed tees side-by-side:
  - LEFT: Sharp, clean print (from good artwork)
  - RIGHT: Blurry, pixelated print (from low-res file)
- Zoom in on details (text, edges)

**Styling:** Lay flat on clean surface, good lighting

**Photo specs:** 1200×800px, landscape

---

### 5. **Dark Garment Underbase** (Dark Garment Section)
**What to shoot:**
- Print process on black tee showing:
  - White underbase layer first (just white)
  - Final colors printed on top of white base
- Side-by-side: With underbase vs without

**Photo specs:** 1200×800px, landscape

---

### 6. **Color Separation Layers** (Color Section)
**What to shoot:**
- Screenshot of Illustrator showing:
  - Layers panel with each color on separate layer
  - Label each layer clearly (e.g., "Red", "Blue", "Yellow")
- Alternatively: Physical screens for each color lined up

**Photo specs:** 1000×800px

---

### 7. **Tiny Text Failure Example** (What to Avoid Section)
**What to shoot:**
- Printed example showing text that's too small (blurry, illegible)
- Close-up photo showing the failure
- Overlay with "8pt minimum" guideline

**Photo specs:** 800×600px

---

### 8. **Workspace/Behind the Scenes** (Hero Background)
**What to shoot:**
- Your workshop/studio with screens, press, supplies
- Wide angle showing the full setup
- Clean, organized workspace (aspirational feel)

**Styling:** Natural light or bright overhead, declutter

**Photo specs:** 1920×1080px, landscape (can be hero background)

## Quick Photo Hacks (If You Can't Shoot All)

### Use These Alternatives:

1. **Screen captures from design software**
   - Free and easy to create
   - Show exact steps customers need to follow
   - Annotate with arrows, circles, text overlays

2. **Before/after comparisons**
   - Print the same design from good file vs bad file
   - Photograph side-by-side
   - High impact, low effort

3. **Process shots during regular work**
   - Keep your phone handy during production
   - Snap photos of interesting moments
   - Natural lighting, authentic feel

4. **Flat lay product shots**
   - Lay finished prints on clean surface
   - Shoot from directly above
   - Use natural window light

## Photo Editing Guidelines

### Specs for Web:
- **Format:** JPG or WebP
- **Max file size:** 200KB per image (compress with TinyPNG)
- **Resolution:** 72 DPI for web (convert from 300 DPI after shooting)
- **Dimensions:** See individual specs above

### Editing Checklist:
- [ ] Crop to remove distractions
- [ ] Adjust brightness/contrast for clarity
- [ ] Add annotations if instructional (arrows, circles, text)
- [ ] Compress for web (<200KB)
- [ ] Save with descriptive filename: `screen-printing-resolution-check.jpg`

## Equipment Needed

- **Camera:** iPhone/Android (12MP+ camera is fine)
- **Lighting:** Natural window light OR overhead shop lights
- **Tripod:** Optional but helpful for flat lays
- **Background:** Clean surface (white paper, clean table)
- **Editing:** Free tools like Canva, Photoshop, or Pixlr

---

## Time Estimate

- **Shooting:** 2-3 hours (can be done during regular production)
- **Editing:** 1-2 hours
- **Adding to blog:** 30 minutes

**Total:** Half a workday, spread across 1-2 weeks

## Quick Win Alternative

**Can't shoot professional photos yet?**

1. Use screenshots from design software (free, easy)
2. Add 2-3 process shots from your phone
3. Use one hero image of your workspace
4. Publish the blog NOW with basic images
5. Replace with better photos over time

**The content matters more than perfect photos.** Get it published first, improve visuals later.
